dead letter queue(dlq):- 
A dead-letter queue (DLQ) is a temporary storage location for messages that a software system can't process or deliver. DLQs are used to store messages that:
Are sent to a non-existent queue
Exceed the size limit
Exceed the maximum queue length
Reach the time to live (TTL)
Are rejected by another queue exchange
Have been read and rejected too many times
